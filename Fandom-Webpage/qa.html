How did you become a fan?

Since I was the young age of 8 (36 now), I enjoyed a great story, about the underdog, who constantly struggled to overcome obstacles while always remaining positive. Over time, these characters evolve, learn life lessons, and became sort of a role model for how I wanted to live as a person. Anime can tell stories in a way that feels unique compared to most other media. They can be very heavy, intense, thrilling, mind-bending, and the art style gives creators more freedom to visualize complex ideas, a nd the best series don’t hold back emotionally. These are some of the best stories with the biggest twist.

What do you love most about it?

What I love most is the combination of mind-bending concepts with real heart. When a story has twists and high stakes but also meaningful character growth, it feels like more than entertainment—it feels like something you can touch, and become. I truly learn from them and sometimes the characters can shape the way I want to live. I especially enjoy anime that has wisdom in it, where the characters are forced to make difficult choices and the story doesn’t pretend every outcome can be perfect.

What would you recommend to someone just getting started?

I’d recommend starting with one series that matches your interests and sticking with it long enough for the story to build. If you like time travel and emotional payoff, Steins;Gate is a great start. If you want strategy, moral complexity, and a powerful ending, try Code Geass. If you prefer shorter arcs with mystery, emotion, and strong twists, Link Click is a great entry point. The main advice is not to judge too quickly. Many of the best anime build slowly and hit harder later.

Do you have any favorite fan communities or creators?

I enjoy fan communities that focus on discussion and analysis, especially where people break down story themes, character decisions, and hidden details. Reddit communities can be great for that (and YouTube analysis channels too), because you can see multiple interpretations of the same ending or character choice. I also respect fan artists and editors who capture the emotional tone of a series—those creations often highlight what made the story memorable in the first place.

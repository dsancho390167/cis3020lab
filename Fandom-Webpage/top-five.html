Steins;Gate

Steins;Gate is one of my favorites because it blends time travel with emotional consequences in a way that feels believable and heavy. The story starts with Okabe as an eccentric, sarcastic “mad scientist,” but the deeper the timeline crisis gets, the more serious and emotionally broken he becomes. I also love the moral dilemma at the center of it: sometimes life forces you into impossible choices, and no timeline gives you everything.
